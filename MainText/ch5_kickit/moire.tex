\section{Reciprocal lattice}

\begin{align}\label{eqn:recip_latt}
    \mathbf{b}_1 &= 2\pi\frac{\mathbf{a}_2\times\mathbf{a}_3}{\mathbf{a}_1\cdot\mathbf{a}_2\times\mathbf{a}_3} \\
    \mathbf{b}_2 &= 2\pi\frac{\mathbf{a}_3\times\mathbf{a}_1}{\mathbf{a}_2\cdot\mathbf{a}_3\times\mathbf{a}_1} \\
    \mathbf{b}_3 &= 2\pi\frac{\mathbf{a}_1\times\mathbf{a}_2}{\mathbf{a}_3\cdot\mathbf{a}_1\times\mathbf{a}_2}
\end{align}


We can construct any arbitrary lattice by a combination of periodic structures. To construct a general lattice, it can be formed as a superposition of individual waves as
    \begin{equation}
        U = \displaystyle\sum\limits_i A e^{\text{i}\mathbf{k}\dot\mathbf{r}}
    \end{equation}
where $A$ is the wave amplitude, $\mathbf{k}$ is the wavevector and $\mathbf{r}$ the space over which it spans.
